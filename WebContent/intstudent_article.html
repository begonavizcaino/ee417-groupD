Your Mental Health

Living and studying in a new country is normally a highly positive and life changing experience. However, sometimes the challenges that come with moving to a different country can be very stressful. Sometimes we suffer in silence unnecessarily, when the best way to deal with a problem is by asking for help.

Non-Emergency Medical Treatment

Normally when you are sick or need medical treatment, you should visit a doctor (known as a general practitioner or GP in Ireland). GPs provide a broad range of services to their patients on all health issues, and may refer patients to see specialists or hospital consultants if required. GP clinics are normally open during the daytime and early evening.

Language Difficulties

Some international students can experience language difficulties and problems understanding accents at first. Likewise, lecturers and domestic students might experience difficulties adjusting to your accent!

To alleviate these problems, it may be useful to try speaking more slowly and clearly than you usually would and, likewise, if you have difficulty understanding other people in your class (or the lecturer), it is quite acceptable to approach the tutor or the lecturer, explain your problem, and ask that they do the same for you.
